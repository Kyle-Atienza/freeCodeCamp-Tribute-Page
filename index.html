Gina Lopez

1953 - 2019

  • Cabinet Secretary
  • Executive
  • Environmentalist
  • Philantrophist

This former Environment and Natural Resources Secretary spent her time in office fighting openpit mining, among many other environmental concerns.

This former Environment and Natural Resources Secretary spent her time in office fighting openpit mining, among many other environmental concerns. Her many achievements include rehabilitating Pasig River and transforming the La Mesa Watershed into a park.

One such visionary is Gina Lopez. She may have been called a lot of glowing names but there were also not a few people who found her eccentric, to say the very least. Stubborn and unreasonable to some. “Na buang na” was what one Facebook commenter posted when footage of her singing “I Believe I Can Fly” made the rounds of social media. But it is undeniable: Gina Lopez was one extraordinary person.

Her Story

Involvements

Bantay Bata

1997

She initiated Bantay Bata 163, the country's first media-based hotline. In 1997, Bantay Bata was the United Nations Grand Awardee for Excellence besting 187 countries all over the world.

Bantay Kalikasan

1997

She established Bantay Kalikasan, for which she received the 1997 International Public Relations Award of Excellence for the Environment and outstanding Manilans Award for the Environment, 2009.

Siniswela

She produced educational television shows on Science, Math, Values, History and English for elementary and Philippine Literature for high school. For Sine'skwela, Ms. Lopez was honored with the UNESCo Kalinga Award, the first Southeast Asian to earn such a distinction.

ABS-CBN Bayan Foundation

She was also the Vice-Chairperson of the ABS-CBN Bayan Foundation, which provides microfinance assistance to micro-entrepreneurs. She was also the Chairman Emeritus of the Southeast Asian Children's Television.

Kapit Bisig para sa Ilog Pasig

Lopez also initiated the rehabilitation of the Pasig River and nearby urban streams through the Kapit Bisig para sa Ilog Pasig project.[9] once considered as the lifeline of the Filipino nation, the Pasig River is one of the most polluted and toxic river systems in the Philippines today. For her efforts in rehabilitating the river, she was appointed in 2010 by President Benigno Aquino III as the Chairperson of the Pasig River Rehabilitation Commission. Her efforts in the commission led to a river rehabilitation revolution which cleansed numerous tributaries in the Pasig river system. She was also responsible for the reforestation of the La Mesa Watershed Reservation, the last remaining forest zone in Metro Manila.

Banyanijuan

She organized Bayanijuan (lit. 'Country of Juan'). Lopez also launched the Save Palawan Movement along with her partner organizations and gathered 7 million signatures for a petition for protection of key biodiversity areas and against mining.[8] Lopez was a vehement anti-mining advocate, known for her stance against large-scale mining in the province of Palawan. She backed the No Mining In Palawan Movement.[11] She also objected to the use of fossil fuels as sources of energy and coal mining.
